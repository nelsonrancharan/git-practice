It's okay to not know, what you don't know!

When you first start coding, its a completely new world you're diving into. It may seem as if every new lesson is completely foreign to you, that's because it is! Don't worry that you aren't experiencing any type of familiarity with the subject matter, or that you feel you spend more time trying to comprehend each subject, rather than moving forward smoothly. Being forced to further explore the copious internal questions you encur, enhances your recall memory on subjects you never even knew about before you started.

Confusion, therefore, is a sign that you are struggling to comprehend something, and your brain is searching for the answers. It's prompts you to look further, to explore more than you even though possible. If you don't have questions while you're learning, then are you even really learning?

The Mountain of Wonder.

When you first start learning something new, like coding, you can feel like sometimes you're just going through the motions. Following step-by-step, but unsure whether you really understand the whole picture. That's because you don't yet, each step gets you closer though. Each step is another building block that you continue to climb, making that giant mountain of knowledge more attainable. Before you know it, you're at the top of the mountain basking in all the things you accomplished, in complete awe of the fact that you made it, and you hunger for more.

This is a normal part of the process of learning a new technology, and in fact, it's a critical part of the process.

When you start a new learning path such as coding or programming, remember that you are essentially having to train your brain to recognize something new. Just like you would have done as a child in school, though it may not have felt the same.
