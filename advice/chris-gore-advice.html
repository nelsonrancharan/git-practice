Hi, my name is Chris, and I have some advice for you!

Peristence is key!

Learning how to code is literally learning a new language. As much as the language apps out there would lead you to believe, you cannot learn a language in only 10 minutes per day. The best years for language learning are the childhood years, and you are probably an adult.

But do not give up hope! Coding is easier than learning a foreign language, and daily practice pays off. It is better to do as much as you can every day than it is to skip many days and try to work for 24 hours straight on the weekend. Your eyes and legs will thank you.

Have reasonable expectations.

I have run into several people recently who have wanted to learn a new skill but get extremely frustrated when they are not proficient in it immediately. It is ok to be horrible at something while you are still learning, and mistakes are the greatest teachers. The fear of being seen as incompetent or weak should not stop you from pursuing your goals.

All good work takes time. Anything worth doing takes effort. There is value in the struggle.
