Hi, my name is Mark and I have some advice for you!

I was in the Navy, a lifetime ago, and I went through the Nuclear Power Program. I was only 20 years old, so obviously, I had no previous training to operate and repair a nuclear reactor. I learned to take great notes and to refer back to them every day, until I had commited them to memory. The program was only a 6 month school, so you can imagine the amount of information I had to retain in a short period of time.

Look back on what you have learned.

Learning would not be complete without looking back and assessing what you have learned. This all starts with taking notes. This is extremely important â€” I cannot stress this point enough. Taking notes is how I keep track of the things I have learned and it allows me to have a great reference point to go back to when I get stuck.

I also found that it is important to schedule time to look over your notes and see whether you remember the material that you have learned.

My most important advice.

Do not try to memorize everything ... instead ... learn to understand it!

When you are first starting out learning to code online, take small steps. Start with the basics and try to understand the concepts behind the things you are learning before moving on.
